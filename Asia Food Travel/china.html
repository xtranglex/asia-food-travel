WONTONS (馄饨)

A Wonton is a type of dumpling similar to a ravioli in Cantonese cuisine, it is filled with shrimp and minced pork and is most commonly served with thin noodles to make wonton noodles. It can also be fried and be served as a dimsum dish. Click HERE for more information.

DUMPLINGS (水餃)

A dumpling is made from a thin sheet of dough consisting of diced vegetables and minced meat which is typically pork however, there are different varieties of dumplings. It can be served either boiled, steamed or pan-fried and usually served in dim sum or as a side dish in small portions along with a chinese vinegar dipping sauce. Click HERE for more information.

PEKING DUCK (北京烤鸭)

Peking Duck is a dish which was originated since the imperial era. The meat is characterized by its thin, crisp skin. Authentic versions of the dish are served mostly with skin and little meat, and sliced in front of the diners by the cook. Click HERE for more information.

MAPO TOFU (麻婆豆腐)

Mapo tofu is a popular Chinese dish from Sichuan province. It consists of tofu set in a spicy sauce made with douchi (fermented black beans) and doubanjiang (broad bean paste) typically thin and oily. Usually served along with minced meat such as pork or beef. Click HERE for more information.

DAN DAN MIAN (担担面)

Dan dan mian is a dish originating from Sichuan cuisine. It consists of a spicy sauce containing preserved vegetables, chili oil, Sichuan pepper, minced pork, and scallions served over noodles. Click HERE for more information

DIMSUM (点心)

Dim sum is a style of Chinese cuisine prepared as small bite-sized portions of food served in small steamer baskets or on small plates. Dim sum dishes are usually eaten with tea. The drinking of tea is as important to dim sum as the food; the type of tea to serve on the table is typically one of the first things the server asks dining customers.A traditional dim sum brunch includes various types of steamed or fried dimsum such as buns, dumplings and noodles. Click HERE for more information.

CONGEE (粥)

Congee is a type of rice porridge popular in many Asian countries, usually with minced pork and century egg but other items can be added. When eaten as plain rice congee, it is most often served with side dishes. It can often be the main choice of food for people who are sick. HERE for more information.

ZHAJIANGMIAN (炸酱面)

Zhajiangmian is a Chinese dish consisting of thick wheat noodles topped with sauce. The sauce is called Zhajiang sauce made from fermented soybean paste and is simmered with minced pork or beef. Click HERE for more information

CHAR SIU (叉燒)

Char Siu is barbecued pork marinated in a sweet sauce made from red food colouring, hoisin sauce, dark soy sauce, honey and five spiced powder.There are common cuts of pork such as pork belly, pork shoulder and pork loin roasted which is served whole or sliced into thin pieces and eaten with rice or as a filling inside a bun.
